FAQS

The following are a collection of questions and answers which we, as an organization, find ourselves recieving the most by people intrigued about our Animal Research and Rescue Center.

What happens to the animals which you rescue?
The animals which we rescue are taken good care of in our facility by our highly trained staff. They are fed and slowly adjusted to their natural enviroment which we prepare them for so they are not lost or struggle to survive on their own. In certain special cases if the animal is in the endangered species list we tend to keep them at our facility to reproduce more and later ensure that the population is at a healthy standpoint before we release them out in the wild.
How can I become a volunteer at this center?
Becoming a volunteer or member at our Animal Research and Rescue Center can simply be done by contacting us via email or by simply calling our facility. All of our contact information can be found on the contact tab of this website. You can also make an appointment with us to visit our facility in which we will provide you with a tour guide to learn more in depth about our animals and facilitation.
Is there any fee that is required to become a volunteer or to visit this center?
There is no required fee to become a volunteer at our center as we see your desire to help take care of our animals as a payment in itself. To visit our center there is also no required fee, however there is a donation option which visitors are open to providing for since all this money will simply be going to help the animals and our research on them.
What are some of my responsibilities as a Volunteer?
As a volunteer you will simply aid the staff with some of the less strenuous and difficult tasks which only our well trained faculty will be dealing with. Volunteers will help with more basic errand related tasks. As volunteers you will not be allowed to take full care of animals by yourself as we do not want untrained hands dealing with them, but your help will be crucial nonetheless.
What is the difference between becoming a volunteer and a member?
A volunteer as stated in the question above will be running small errands, but still extremely crucial. We do not driscrimante against age, however a parent or guardian must accompany any minor who wishes to volunteer at our facility. Becoming a member requires such person(s) to be at least 18 years of age and is already trained with animal research and rescue or is willing to be trained by our faculty to perform more labor intensive tasks.
