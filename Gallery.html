This is Swiper who is a baby Red Wolf pup which we rescued out in the wild and is currently kept in our center as his kind is limited out in the wild.

This is an image of Screech, the Long-Eared Owl on the left and Houdini, the Short-Eared on the right. Neither of these animals were hurt when we found them, however at our facility we did not have much research on either of the two species of owls so we took them in. Houdini will most likely stay with us as his kind are endangered.

This is a baby Black-Footed Ferret who was born in our facility. This is another of the species whose population is low in the wild meaning we will be taking care of her at our facility. She does not have a name yet so you are more than welcome to send us name ideas through our email on the contact page.

This is Dash who is a young Golden Eagle which we found struggling to fly as one of his wings seemed to have been injured as he was visibly strugling to open his right wing.

This is Medusa the Queen Snake who as we were on the look out for more reptiles to do research on, we saw her slithering her way into our camp site. She made all her way near the container in which we kept our bait in case of luring any sort of species around if we struggled to do so naturally.

This tiny little creature thats waving at you is Piplup who is a Diamondback Terrapin. The small stature of these species is why they are on the endangered species list as the shell size for a male grows only up to 5.5 inches while females reach to 11 inches. Piplup here can easily be picked up using only your index finger and thumb.
